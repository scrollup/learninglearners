About The Center

Learning Learners has grown along with the children we’ve been supporting these past few years. As the needs of the children grow, so have we expanded to include everything your child needs. Our most recent addition is the opening of a center in the heart of Crown Heights, on the beautiful Eastern Parkway right across the street from Oholei Torah Yeshiva.

We’re always about providing support that goes full circle and accommodates student, provider, and family. Located at 678 Eastern Parkway, the center provides the opportunity for students to relax and learn at their own pace without external distractions. We are fully stocked with healthy snacks, water, and even hot cocoa!

The center also has a kitchen, which provides for the opportunity for extra-curricular baking classes as well as a room for art. Please reach out if you would feel that your child can gain from these activities.

We view the process through the eyes of a child and the heart of a parent. Slow and steady, without boundaries and with lots of love. We understand the struggles associated with the complexities of special ed and hold your hand throughout the process.

Our Services

P3/ SETSS (Special Education Teacher Support Services)

Our staff of licensed Special Educators are assigned to children with SETSS mandates. They will assess the child, create appropriate goals, and a proper treatment plan to ensure success in the mainstream classroom or in the home setting.

Paraprofessional Services

These services help students with special needs fully take part in all school activities. Our paras work with the student to ensure that health and learning needs are being met at schools.

SEIT [Special Education Itinerant Teacher]

Please contact for details.

Counseling

Together with your input, we will assign a member of our team of Licensed, highly trained counseling providers for your child.

Social Groups

Groups play a basic role in the development of social nature. In a social group, our highly trained facilitator teaches the students how to interact appropriately with others their age, improve conversational, conflict resolution, joint attention, and friendship skills in a small setting. Additional skills such as active listening, empathy, and respect are taught, practiced, and reviewed in a safe setting before implementation in the real social world.
